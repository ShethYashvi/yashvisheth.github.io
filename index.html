Research

Heavy Metal Remediation from water

Contamination of water has been alarming due to the increasing industrialization and disposal of inefficiently treated wastewater. The use of conventional technologies has certain disadvantages like disposal of secondary waste, larger pressure drops in columns, etc. This creates an urgent need of having a technology that overcomes the above drawbacks. To overcome the drawbacks, we have come up with a novel strategy to synthesize nanocomposites by impregnating nanoparticles onto a rigid surface that would offer extraordinary efficiencies for the removal of the heavy metal ions from water in a continuous mode. This technique not only incorporates the advantages of the nanoparticles, but would also provide lower pressure drops in column. The substrate for impregnation chosen is such that nanoparticles have strong binding with it and hence, nanoparticles are not washed away. My PhD work involves developing a state-of-art composite that will be used for removal of the metal from water. Apart from this, the experimental work would be modelled to get the better insights into the process.
